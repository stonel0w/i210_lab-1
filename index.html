Hometown: Fishers, IN

Location

Fishers is located about 30 minutes northeast of Indianapolis. It's neighboring cities are Westfield, Noblesville, and Carmel. Fishers is in the southeast section of Hamilton County, alongside the west side of the White River. Near the southeastern part of the city, (where i grew up), lies the Geist Reservoir, which covers three counties and contains about 6,100,000,000 gallons of water.

About

Geist Reservoir

Geist Reservoir is a massive manmade body of water in the southeastern section of Fishers. At the time of it's completion in the 1940s, it was the world's second largest manmade body of water. Today, Geist is famous for the magnificient lakeside houses and resturaunts, the boating club, as well as the vibrant community of passionate bass fishermen.

City History

Fishers was first settled in 1802. Settlers began to move into the area after Indiana's statehood was granted in 1816. The name "Fishers" comes from Fishers' Switch, a railroad stop built in the area in 1891. The name Fishers was officially granted in 1908. The initial population in the early 1960s was only about 340 people, but as the town grew, so did it's people. By 2010, Fishers had a population of about 38,000, and officially became a city from a town in 2012.

Connor Prairie

When Fishers was initially settled in 1802, the founder, William Conner, built a log cabin and trading post alongside the White River. Today, this intiial location and cabin is now surrounded by Conner Prairie, a living historical museum established in 1934. The museum is famous for it's historical accuracy with character actors re-enacting what life was like for settlers in the early 18th century alongside the White River. It also hosts many summer programs and camps, as well as outdoor music venues with the Indianapolis Symphony Orchestra.

Things to Do

  • Visit the beautiful downtown Municipal Complex
  • Walk along the historical Nickel Plate district
  • Experience history at Conner Prairie's museums
  • Shop around the small businesses that thrive in Fishers
  • Enjoy the fresh air and great weather at the various parks and lakes
